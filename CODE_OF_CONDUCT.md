# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for all community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

---

## Our Standards

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Helping new contributors feel welcome
- Acknowledging and crediting contributions
- Supporting fellow developers in learning and growth

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, or religion
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Personal attacks on maintainers or contributors
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:
- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

Community spaces include:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, code comments)
- Email communications
- Public forums and chat channels
- Social media posts
- Any other community communication channels

---

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

**Email**: Contact the repository owner at the email provided in their GitHub profile

**GitHub Issue**: Open a confidential issue describing the incident (if possible without exposing sensitive information)

**Direct Message**: Contact a maintainer directly with details

### What to Include in Your Report

Please provide:
- A detailed description of the incident
- Names of individuals involved (if known)
- Any relevant context or screenshots
- How the behavior affected you
- Whether you've discussed it with those involved (if safe to do so)
- What action you're requesting

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

---

## Confidentiality

We are committed to protecting the privacy and safety of those who report incidents. Reporter information will be kept confidential to the extent legally possible.

---

## Consequences for Violations

Depending on the severity of the violation, community leaders may take action including but not limited to:

1. **Warning**: A private message explaining the violation and expected behavior change
2. **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces (24 hours to 7 days)
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from community spaces and contributions

---

## Appeals Process

If you believe you've been unfairly treated or sanctioned, you may:

1. Request a formal review within 7 days of the action
2. Provide additional context or evidence
3. Have your case reviewed by maintainers
4. Receive a written response explaining the decision

## Examples

### Acceptable Behavior

```
"I disagree with your approach. Have you considered using X instead? 
Here's why I think it might work better..."

"Thank you for contributing! While we can't merge this PR as-is, 
your effort is appreciated. Here's what we'd like to see changed..."

"Welcome to the project! If you need any help getting started, 
I'm happy to assist."
```

### Unacceptable Behavior

```
"Your code is trash. You clearly don't understand Java."

"Women shouldn't be coding. Go back to the kitchen."

"I'm sharing your home address because you disagreed with me."

"Let's organize a harassment campaign against this contributor."
```
